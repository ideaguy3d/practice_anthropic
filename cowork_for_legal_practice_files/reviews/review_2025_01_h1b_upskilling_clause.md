# Legal Review: H-1B Upskilling Mandate Clause
**Date:** January 14, 2025
**Reviewed by:** Legal Team
**Matter:** American Worker Protection Act — Draft Section 4(b)
**Status:** Concluded — Approved with conditions

## Question Reviewed
Does Section 4(b) of the draft AWPA — requiring employers to offer upskilling to any American worker before filing an H-1B petition — create an undue burden that would expose the legislation to Commerce Clause challenge?

## Conclusion
Section 4(b) is defensible under the Commerce Clause provided the upskilling window is explicitly capped (recommended: 90–180 days) and the employer's good-faith effort is documented through a federal registry. An uncapped mandate would likely be challenged as an unconstitutional restraint on interstate commerce.

**Condition:** The registry mechanism (AmericanLaborDefense.org or equivalent) must be codified in the statute, not delegated entirely to regulation.

## Key Authorities Cited
- Gonzales v. Raich, 545 U.S. 1 (2005) — broad Commerce Clause authority over labor markets
- United States v. Lopez, 514 U.S. 549 (1995) — limits on federal labor regulation without economic nexus
- INA Section 212(n) — existing good-faith recruitment requirements for H-1B

## Open Issues at Time of Review
- Penalty structure not yet drafted (deferred to Phase 2)
- Definition of "qualified American worker" needs regulatory guidance
- AI-assisted upskilling timeline not addressed in draft
