# Legal Review: Registry Verification Mechanism
**Date:** May 9, 2025
**Reviewed by:** Legal Team
**Matter:** American Worker Protection Act — AmericanLaborDefense.org Integration
**Status:** Concluded — Approved

## Question Reviewed
Can AmericanLaborDefense.org be designated in federal statute as the official verification registry, or must the registry be a government-operated system?

## Conclusion
Federal statute may designate a privately operated registry as an official compliance tool provided: (1) the government retains audit authority over the registry, (2) data standards and retention requirements are codified, and (3) the registry operator is subject to federal data privacy law. This structure is analogous to E-Verify, which relies on private employer participation but is government-controlled.

**Approved** with the recommendation that AmericanLaborDefense.org be structured as a federally chartered nonprofit or operated under a federal contract to insulate it from Commerce Clause and nondelegation challenges.

## Key Authorities Cited
- Whitman v. American Trucking Assns, 531 U.S. 457 (2001) — nondelegation doctrine limits
- E-Verify statutory framework, 8 U.S.C. Section 1324a — model for private registry with federal oversight
- Privacy Act, 5 U.S.C. Section 552a — data protection standards

## Open Issues at Time of Review

- Appeals process for disputed registry determinations not drafted
- Cross-agency data sharing (DOL, DHS, USCIS) needs MOU framework
