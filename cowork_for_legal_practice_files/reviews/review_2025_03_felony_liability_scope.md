# Legal Review: Criminal Felony Liability — Scope of Persons
**Date:** March 3, 2025
**Reviewed by:** Legal Team
**Matter:** American Worker Protection Act — Draft Section 7
**Status:** Concluded — Flagged for revision

## Question Reviewed
Does imposing individual felony liability on HR personnel, hiring managers, and C-suite executives (in addition to the corporate entity) create double jeopardy or conflict with existing corporate criminal liability doctrine?

## Conclusion
No double jeopardy issue — corporate liability and individual liability are separate and well-established in federal criminal law (Responsible Corporate Officer Doctrine). However, the draft as written creates ambiguity: it is unclear whether a hiring manager who followed explicit C-suite direction is equally liable as the executive who issued the order.

**Flagged for revision:** Add a "chain of command" provision. An employee acting under documented executive direction should face reduced exposure; the executive bears primary liability.

## Key Authorities Cited
- United States v. Park, 421 U.S. 658 (1975) — Responsible Corporate Officer Doctrine
- Yates v. United States, 574 U.S. 528 (2015) — individual liability in corporate crime
- 18 U.S.C. Section 3571 — federal criminal fine structure

## Open Issues at Time of Review
- Mens rea standard not specified (knowing vs. willful)
- Whistleblower protection provision recommended but not drafted
- Statute of limitations not addressed
