# Internal Memo: New Policy Feature — AI Upskilling Certification Requirement
**Date:** June 1, 2026
**From:** Policy Team
**To:** Legal Team
**Re:** Proposed addition to Section 4(b) — AI Tool Certification

## Summary
The policy team is proposing to add a requirement that employers use a federally approved AI upskilling platform during the mandatory upskilling window. The question for legal: does this new requirement reopen the January 2025 Commerce Clause review of Section 4(b)?

## What's New
Original Section 4(b) required employers to offer upskilling but was silent on the method. The proposed amendment would require employers to use a federally approved AI platform (a pre-certified list maintained by the Department of Labor).

## Key Question for Legal
Does mandating a specific class of upskilling tools (federally approved AI platforms) change the constitutional analysis from the January 14, 2025 review? Specifically:
- Does it create a new Commerce Clause issue (federal government favoring certain vendors)?
- Does it implicate the nondelegation doctrine (DOL maintaining an approved vendor list)?
- Does it conflict with the May 2025 registry review conclusions?

## Requested Action
Please run /brief on this question against the January 2025 and May 2025 reviews before we proceed to drafting.
