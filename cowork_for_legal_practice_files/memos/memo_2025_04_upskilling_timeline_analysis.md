# Internal Memo: Upskilling Timeline — 1 to 6 Month Window
**Date:** April 22, 2025
**From:** Legal Research
**To:** Policy Team
**Re:** Defensibility of 1–6 Month Upskilling Mandate

## Summary
The proposed 1–6 month upskilling window is legally defensible and practically supported by modern AI-assisted training capabilities.

## Legal Defensibility
A mandatory upskilling window before an employer may petition for a foreign worker is analogous to the existing PERM labor certification process, which already requires employers to conduct good-faith U.S. worker recruitment before hiring foreign nationals. The AWPA upskilling window is more structured and time-bound, which arguably makes it more legally defensible than the current vague "good faith" standard.

Recommended statutory language: "An employer shall offer upskilling to any American worker who expresses interest in the role within 30 days of posting. The upskilling period shall not exceed 180 days. An employer may petition for a foreign worker only upon written certification that no American worker completed upskilling within the mandated period."

## Practical Support
Modern AI-assisted training platforms can compress skill acquisition timelines significantly. Engineering roles that previously required 12–18 months of on-the-job ramp-up can often be completed in 4–8 weeks with structured AI tutoring. This supports the 1–6 month window as reasonable rather than aspirational.

## Risk
The primary litigation risk is an employer arguing the upskilling window constitutes an unconstitutional "taking" of their right to hire. This argument is weak — employment decisions are not property rights — but should be addressed in the legislative findings section.
